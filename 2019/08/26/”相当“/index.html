“相当”

Aug 26, 2019

This is a non-descriptive qualifier, similar to fairly and rather and somewhat. Used where a plain adjective needs to be modified, but cannot be qualified. When spoken, the meaning can vary with the tone of voice and stress. He was quite big can mean anything from “not exactly small” to “almost huge”. More typically used sarcastically in the UK, where “quite clever” has negative connotations vs. in the US where it would mean “very smart”.—wiki
Translations
to the greatest extent; completely
in a fully justified sense; truly
to a moderate extent
在中文中：
(1) [quite;fairly;considerably]∶很;十分;极 这里相当清静。
(2) [enough]∶足可以,尚 穿这件衣服去参加晚会就已经相当好啦。
在德语中：
ziemlich：非常，相当，有点，适度地。也是如此。

所以“相当”可以多也可以少。
PS:相当、ziemlich和quite，都是只能作为修饰adj或者adv的副词，而不能修饰动词。
